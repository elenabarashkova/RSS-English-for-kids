Scrum — is a methodology that helps teams to work together.

Scrum helps to deal with unpredictability and solve complex problems through adaptive solutions.

It organises teams in a way that they would be able to efficiently work together:

  • learn lessons from their experience;
  • get self-organisation skills;
  • work together on problem-solving;
  • analyze all successes and failures in order to develop and improve performance;

Scrum principles

Adaptation - is the main principle and goal of using scrum.

Inspection

Transparency

Scrum Team

3 main role types:

  • And development team

Developers

  • self-managing unit

Product Owner

  • knows how the product works
  • knows how to adjust or improve product in order to meet customer's requirements
  • prioritizes tasks in backlog

Scrum Master

  • implements Scrum into the company

Good sides of scrum:

  • Scrum is oriented on client because of its adaptivity. It gives a client the ability to change requirements any time
  • Scrum is easy in learning and implementation
  • Saves time by the ranked Backlog
  • Scrum is focused on self-organised unit, that is able to complete tasks with minimum coordination from outside of it. That gives on opportunity not to hire some management employers.

Not so good sides of scrum:

  • though scrum is simple, it has some firm rules
  • Scrum can conflict with being client-oriented - when for example client can change the task or the whole concept in the middle of sprint and to be able to fulfill customer's need the company or team would have to stop sprint or change it right in the middle, witch breaks the whole system.
  • It's more difficult to find right members for developments team
  • Scrum originally is created for small companies, and for large groups use adaptation or customisation of scrum
